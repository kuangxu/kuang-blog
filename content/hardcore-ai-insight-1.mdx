## The Constraint No One Is Talking About

Most discussions about AI capability focus on training. More data, more parameters, better
RLHF. But the actual constraint in production is **inference-time compute allocation**.

$$
\text{Quality}(x) = \int_0^T f(\theta, x, t) \, dt
$$

That integral is money. And right now, the market is wildly mispricing how it scales.

## Why This Architecture Matters

When you decouple generation from verification, you unlock something qualitatively different:

```python
def generate_and_verify(prompt: str, budget: int) -> str:
    candidates = [model.generate(prompt) for _ in range(budget)]
    scores = verifier.score_all(candidates)
    return candidates[scores.argmax()]
```

The verifier is the moat. Not the generator.
